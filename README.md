# The Weaver’s Pick Counter
	
Description:

The Weaver’s Pick Counter is a mobile application for collectors of textile antiquities, historians of the garment industry, and preservers of industrial weaving heritage, dedicated to cataloging the specialized magnification and measurement tools used to analyze fabric density and yarn quality that have been replaced by digital textile analyzers and high-speed camera sensors. Systematize your collection of the "eyes of the mill"—from folding brass "louping" glasses used for counting picks and ends to precision yarn reels for measuring thread weight, from hand - cranked twist testers to specialized cloth balances and silver-cased thread counters. Create a digital archive of textile craftsmanship by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, magnification power, era, and the specific mill or weaving tradition each specimen represents. 

## Example Object Fields (The "Textile Instrument" Model):

•	Loom Identifier: Unique code (e.g., "TWPC-MACBETH-1920-LANCASHIRE-047"). 

•	Instrument Type: Category (folding pick counter, yarn twist tester, thread reel, cloth balance, moisture meter, fiber micrometer). 

•	Specific Function: What it measured (thread count per inch, yarn twist direction, fabric weight, fiber diameter). 

•	Manufacturer and Brand: Company name (Alfred Suter, James H. Heal & Co., Macbeth, Uster, Goodbrand & Co). 

•	Country of Origin: Where manufactured (UK—specifically Lancashire/Yorkshire, USA, Switzerland, Germany, India). 

•	Era of Production: Decade of manufacture (1880s, 1910s, 1930s, 1950s). 

•	Magnification / Precision: Power of the lens (e.g., 5x, 10x) or the sensitivity of the balance. 

•	Materials: Primary construction (polished brass, heavy cast iron, vulcanite, glass optics, nickel plating). 

•	Operation Type: Manual folding, hand-cranked, or gravity-based weight measurement. 

•	Dimensions and Weight: Physical size in cm/inches, weight in grams/oz. 

•	Condition: Lens clarity, mechanical movement, original lacquer or paint, scale legibility. 

•	Markings and Stamps: Manufacturer hallmarks, mill inventory numbers, or patent dates. 

•	Provenance: Where found (closed textile mill, tailor's estate, industrial auction, historical society).
